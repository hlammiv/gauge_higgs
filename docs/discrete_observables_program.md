## overview

## The physics picture and the logic of the program

**Goal.** Use a single Higgs in a carefully chosen irrep R of SU(N) whose generic-VEV stabilizer is a target discrete *nonabelian* subgroup H, drive the hopping κ deep into the Higgs region, and demonstrate on the lattice that the long-distance theory is an H gauge theory — the nonabelian generalization of Fradkin–Shenker's U(1)→Z_N.

**The breaking, made concrete (grounded in the team's own draft + notes).**
- **SU(2):** j=3 (dim 7) → binary tetrahedral 2T; j=4 (dim 9) → binary octahedral 2O; j=6 (dim 13) → binary icosahedral 2I. The explicit H-singlet VEV vectors are in `draft-1.tex` Tab. su2singlet. These are **integer-j (odd-dim, real)** irreps, so by the draft's own kernel analysis (`draft-1.tex` §"Orbits…", line 868) **Z₂ ⊂ ker(ρ)**: the Higgs is *center-blind* (N-ality 0) and the residual H *contains the full center Z₂* of SU(2).
- **SU(3):** (2,2)→Σ(108); (4,1)→Σ(216); (3,3)→Σ(648); (6,0)→Σ(1080); plus (1,0)·… for Δ(27) via the 10=(3,0). Explicit singlet VEVs in `draft-1.tex` Tab. su3singlet. For (p+2q)≡0 mod 3, **Z₃ ⊂ ker(ρ)**, again center-blind, H ⊇ Z₃.

**Why this matters for the whole program (the single most important design fact).** Because the chosen Higgs irreps are **center-blind (trivial N-ality)**:
1. Fradkin–Shenker permits a **genuine bulk transition** (not just the fundamental-Higgs crossover) — so the deep-Higgs region can be a *distinct phase*, and the L_link susceptibility/Binder can show a true volume-growing singularity. This is the cleanest possible setting and it is exactly the case the team's reps realize.
2. The residual center is the **full Z(SU(N)) = Z_N**, which **survives unbroken** (the Higgs cannot screen center flux it does not carry). So center-sensitive observables (rep-R′ Polyakov loops, center-twist free energies) are *clean*, not screened away — a major simplification versus generic Higgs reps. The "missing observable #3" worry of the critics (Higgs screening the very center you probe) does **not** bite here, and we should state that as a gating fact.

**The three-legged demonstration.** No single observable proves "deep-Higgs = H gauge theory." The vetted program rests on three mutually-reinforcing legs, all Elitzur-safe:
- **Group-theory order parameters (cheap, run first):** gauge-invariant H-invariant Higgs polynomials P_H[φ] (the Elitzur-safe replacement for ⟨φ⟩) + the L_link/L_φ susceptibility/Binder/FSS phase-diagram map. These locate the transition and confirm the VEV sits on the H-stratum.
- **Electric leg (the bulk of the evidence):** the gauge-boson mass spectrum via FMS composites (Observation 2 of the draft: mass eigenstates = H-charge eigenstates, degeneracies = adj_SU(N)→H branching multiplicities; for SU(3) crystal subgroups all 8 gauge bosons are *degenerate*, M_ab ∝ 1 — a razor-sharp prediction, `draft-1.tex` line 879), plus the rep-R′ Wilson/Polyakov screening table. The "all N²−1 vectors massive, no surviving massless vector" statement is the cleanest discrete-vs-continuous discriminator.
- **Magnetic/deep-Higgs-matching leg (the nonabelian capstone):** match the deep-Higgs screening table, string-tension tower and glueball spectrum to a standalone finite-group H-LGT run in the same code; then, as the genuine nonabelian-discrete hallmark, conjugacy-class-labeled 't Hooft/disorder operators (flux = conjugacy class of H, the quantum-double D(H) structure).

**Honest framing on dimension.** In 4D the residual discrete H gauge theory is itself in a deconfined/perimeter (Coulomb-like) phase, so naive area-vs-perimeter is *not* the discriminator at large distance — constant screening-energy shifts and static-state degeneracies are. We should run the cleanest confining tests in **D=3** (the code's D is a compile-time macro; build an NDIM=3 binary), where the discrete H theory confines its nontrivial irreps and the screening table is sharp, and use 4D for the spectrum/matching.

**Spectroscopy backbone (Observations 1–3, the paper's theorems).**
- **Obs 1:** unique-H-singlet VEV is an extremum of any G-invariant V on |φ|=|φ_VEV| (no fine-tuning).
- **Obs 2:** mass eigenstates = H-charge eigenstates; scalars in the same H-irrep ρ split into at most m_{σ→ρ} masses; if m_{σ→ρ}=1 they are *exactly degenerate* (eq. for λ_ρ via the H-projector). This is the gauge-invariant degeneracy pattern that fingerprints H.
- **Obs 3:** Goldstones are exactly adj_G|_H — they are eaten, so the *massive* vector multiplet structure is (adj_G|_H minus the would-be-Goldstone count is zero here since H discrete ⇒ all of adj is "eaten"/massive): all N²−1 vectors massive, organized into H-multiplets with the branching degeneracies.

## pitfalls

## Pitfalls and traps (vetted)

**Elitzur / gauge-invariance discipline.**
- **Never ⟨φ⟩.** Use H-invariant polynomials P_H[φ] (SU(N) singlets) and FMS composites only. All proposed observables are Elitzur-safe as written; the only gauge-fixed one (Greensite–Matsuyama) is a *legitimate* construction and is held in reserve for non-center-blind reps.
- **FMS composites must be fully color-contracted G-singlets.** A half-contracted "φ-and-link" operator is gauge-variant — a silent Elitzur violation. Make full contraction a hard implementation rule (C, D, G).
- **Global-vs-gauge firewall.** Higgs-bilinear / flavor invariants detect the residual *global* custodial symmetry; the bridge to SU(N)→H is the FMS dictionary, never gauge SSB (which Elitzur forbids). Do not state "the gauge group broke" from a bilinear.
- **Class-projection is the gauge-invariance lynchpin of every nonabelian magnetic operator (K, L).** Flux is labeled by conjugacy *classes*, not elements; quantum fluctuations conjugate a→bab⁻¹, so **only character/class-level quantities are gauge-invariant**. Using D^(ν) matrix elements or element-labeled insertions gives gauge-variant nonsense.

**Interpretation traps.**
- **Casimir-scaling shoulder.** At intermediate r ALL reps Casimir-scale (σ_R′∝C₂) — this is universal and is NOT a fingerprint of H. Only the *asymptotic* (post-string-breaking) behavior fingerprints Z(H)/H-content. Stopping at small loops will fool you (the de Forcrand–Jahn SO(3) negative-adjoint-Polyakov-loop episode is the canonical caution).
- **Dimension dependence of area/perimeter.** In 4D the residual discrete H gauge theory is deconfined (perimeter phase), so H-charged loops *also* show perimeter law and area-vs-perimeter collapses as a discriminator — switch to constant screening-energy shifts + static-state degeneracies, or run the confining tests in **D=3**.
- **Higgs string-breaking mimics H-screening.** The dynamical Higgs breaks strings at the Higgs scale, which can imitate the H-screening selection rule. Disentangle by matching to the Higgs-free H-LGT (where genuine H-screening persists but Higgs-breaking is absent).
- **Polyakov-loop dichotomy is quantitative, not qualitative.** With dynamical matter the loop is never exactly zero (exponentially small in L_t for confined reps). Need large L_t, good statistics, renormalized (Z_R′^{Nt}) or ratio comparisons, never bare cross-rep magnitudes.

**Phase-structure / finite-volume.**
- **True transition vs finite-V artifact.** The historical SU(2)-Higgs "first-order" that became a crossover is the warning. Establish order by FSS (χ∝V at first order vs saturating; Binder dip vs →2/3) and guard against metastability/hysteresis faking first-order via hot/cold starts and multi-histogram/Lee–Yang.
- **Bulk/freezing artifact.** Finite nonabelian subgroups have a first-order freezing (order–disorder) transition; keep the H-LGT match and the deep-Higgs measurement in the same confining window *away* from it, and compare dimensionless ratios (lattice spacings need not agree).

**Magnetic-sector specifics.**
- Disorder free energies are **not single expectation values** — they need ratio-of-partition-functions / reweighting / snake / dynamical-flux HMC; statistically demanding, S/N degrades with volume.
- Twist surfaces must be genuinely **coclosed** (closed dual sheet) or the construction is not gauge-invariant.
- SU(N)\H flux insertions bound a **domain wall** (area law) — diagnostic, but must be interpreted, not mistaken for instability of an H class.
- Cheshire/braiding (L) is a subtle *correlation*, not a one-point function; needs large volumes to separate loops beyond the screening length.

**Code-specific.**
- `DistLattice` is hard-periodic — the entire magnetic program needs a twist-aware BC variant first.
- Only the *fundamental* Polyakov loop exists and it is serial — every other loop is genuinely new code; the "easy rep-R′ loops" framing hides the MPI gather + smearing + GEVP cost.
- Rep engine is O(N^n) per `rep_matrix`; all target reps fit the 200000 cap, but high-rep *probe* R′ for the screening table can be slow — add the cached exp(i ω·T_R) path or pre-check each probe rep's N^n.
- Multi-Higgs (B-matrix family) touches the HMC core (single hardcoded `DistScalar`) — not a small extension; defer.
